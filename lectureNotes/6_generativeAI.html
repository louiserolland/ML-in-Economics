Variational Autoencoders (VAE)

Prince (2023, chap. 17)

Latent Variable Models

\[\begin{align*} P(\mathbf{x}) = \int P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z})P(\mathbf{z})d\mathbf{z} \end{align*}\]

Nonlinear Latent Variable Model

VAEs aim to represent \(P(\mathbf{x})\) with nonlinear latent variable model \[\begin{align*} P(\mathbf{z}) &= \mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{z}}(0,\mathbf{I})\\ P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z},\phi) &= \mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{x}}(f[\mathbf{z},\phi],\sigma^2 \mathbf{I}) \end{align*}\] where \(f[z,\phi]\) will be the decoder network.

Generation: Ancestral Sampling

Maximizing Log-Likelihood (Evidence)

\[\begin{align*} \hat{\phi} &= \arg\max_{\phi} \sum_{i=1}^I \log P(\mathbf{x}_i|\phi) \\ &= \arg\max_{\phi} \sum_{i=1}^I \log \left( \int \mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{x}_i}(f[\mathbf{z},\phi],\sigma^2 \mathbf{I})\mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{z}}(0,\mathbf{I})d\mathbf{z} \right) \end{align*}\]

Intractable integral prevents maximum likelihood learning.

Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO) on Log-Likelihood

\[\begin{align*} \log(P(\mathbf{x}|\phi)) &= \log\left(\int q(\mathbf{z}|\theta) \frac{P(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}|\phi)}{q(\mathbf{z}|\theta)} d\mathbf{z} \right) \\ &\geq \int q(\mathbf{z}|\theta) \log\left(\frac{P(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}|\phi)}{q(\mathbf{z}|\theta)}\right) d\mathbf{z} =: ELBO(\theta,\phi) \end{align*}\] due to Jensen’s inequality. \(\theta\) parametrizes lower bound.

Jensen’s Inequality

ELBO = Log-Likelihood – KL-Div. to Posterior

\[\begin{align*} ELBO(\theta,\phi) &= \int q(\mathbf{z}|\theta) \log\left(\frac{P(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x},\phi)P(\mathbf{x}|\phi)}{q(\mathbf{z}|\theta)}\right) d\mathbf{z} \\ &= \int q(\mathbf{z}|\theta) \log(P(\mathbf{x}|\phi)) d\mathbf{z} + \int q(\mathbf{z}|\theta) \log\left(\frac{P(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x},\phi)}{q(\mathbf{z}|\theta)}\right) d\mathbf{z} \\ &= \log(P(\mathbf{x}|\phi)) - D_{KL}(q(\mathbf{z}|\theta)||P(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x},\phi)) \end{align*}\] Maximizing w.r.t. \(\theta\) (encoder) tightens bound, pushing towards \(q(\mathbf{z}|\theta)\approxeq P(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x},\phi)\).

Maximizing w.r.t. \(\phi\) (decoder) maximizes on that bound.

Variational Approximation

Posterior \[\begin{align*} P(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x},\phi) = \frac{P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z},\phi)P(\mathbf{z})}{P(\mathbf{x}|\phi)} \end{align*}\] also intractable (\(P(\mathbf{x}|\phi)\)) but variational approximation with parametric \(q(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x},\theta)\), e.g. \(q(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x},\theta)=\mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{z}}(\mathbf{g}_{\mathbf{\mu}}[\mathbf{x},\mathbf{\theta}], \mathbf{g}_{\mathbf{\Sigma}}[\mathbf{x},\mathbf{\theta}])\).

Posterior Distribution over Latent Variable

ELBO = Reconstr. Loss – KL-Div. to Prior

\[\begin{align*} & ELBO(\theta,\phi) = \int q(\mathbf{z}|\theta) \log\left(\frac{P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z},\phi)P(\mathbf{z})}{q(\mathbf{z}|\theta)}\right) d\mathbf{z} \\ &= \int q(\mathbf{z}|\theta) \log(P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z},\phi)) d\mathbf{z} + \int q(\mathbf{z}|\theta) \log\left(\frac{P(\mathbf{z})}{q(\mathbf{z}|\theta)}\right) d\mathbf{z} \\ &= \int q(\mathbf{z}|\theta) \log(P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z},\phi)) d\mathbf{z} - D_{KL}(q(\mathbf{z}|\theta)||P(\mathbf{z})) \end{align*}\] where the first term measures reconstruction loss (how well latent variable reconstructs data).

Variational Autoencoder (VAE)

Encoder network computing mean & variance of the variational posterior \[\begin{align*} q(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x},\theta) = \mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{z}}(\mathbf{g}_{\mathbf{\mu}}[\mathbf{x},\mathbf{\theta}], \mathbf{g}_{\mathbf{\Sigma}}[\mathbf{x},\mathbf{\theta}]) \end{align*}\] permits computing and maximizing1 (w.r.t. \(\phi\) and \(\theta\)) the ELBO (for data \(\mathbf{x}\)) \[\begin{align*} ELBO(\phi,\theta) &= \int q(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x},\theta) \log(P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z},\phi)) d\mathbf{z} - D_{KL}(q(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x},\theta)||P(\mathbf{z})) \\ &\approxeq \log(P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z}^*,\phi)) -\frac{1}{2} \left(tr(\mathbf{\Sigma}) + \mathbf{\mu}^T\mathbf{\mu} - D_{\mathbf{z}} -\log |\mathbf{\Sigma}| \right) \end{align*}\] where \(\mathbf{z}^* \sim q(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x},\theta)\) serves as a (crude) Monte Carlo estimate of the expectation, and \(D_{\mathbf{z}}\) is the latent space dimension. Recall that \[\begin{align*} P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z}^*,\phi) &= \mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{x}}(f[\mathbf{z}^*,\phi],\sigma^2 \mathbf{I}) \end{align*}\] is determined by decoder network \(f[\mathbf{z}^*,\phi]\).

  1. Optimization as usual with SGD/Adam using gradients of ELBO w.r.t. \(\phi\) and \(\theta\).

Simultaneous Optimization of \(\theta\) and \(\phi\)

VAE Architecture

Reparametrization Trick

To allow backprop, compute sample as \[\begin{align*} \mathbf{z}^* &= \mathbf{\mu} + \mathbf{\Sigma}^{1/2} \mathbf{\varepsilon}^*\\ \mathbf{\varepsilon}^* &\sim \mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{\varepsilon}}(0,\mathbf{I}) \end{align*}\] which is differentiable w.r.t. \(\theta\) (i.e. \(\mathbf{\mu}\) and \(\mathbf{\Sigma}\); backprop need not pass down stochastic branch).

Approxmating Sample Probability with Importance Sampling

\[\begin{align*} P(\mathbf{x}) &= \int P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z})P(\mathbf{z})d\mathbf{z} \\ &= \int \frac{P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z})P(\mathbf{z})}{q(\mathbf{z})} q(\mathbf{z}) d\mathbf{z} \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{q(\mathbf{z})} \left[ \frac{P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z})P(\mathbf{z})}{q(\mathbf{z})} \right]\\ &\approxeq \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n=1}^N \frac{P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z}_n)P(\mathbf{z}_n)}{q(\mathbf{z}_n)} \end{align*}\] where sampling \(\mathbf{z}_n \sim q(\cdot|\mathbf{x},\theta)\) from the variational posterior promises efficient sampling.

With sufficient samples, this may provide better estimate than lower bound.

Application: Generation

High-quality generation by sampling from aggregated posterior \(q(\mathbf{z}|\theta)\), using hierarchical priors and specialized architectures/regularization.

Application: Resynthesis

Vector in latent space representing smiling/open mouth estimated as difference between the mean \(\mathbf{z}\) across the two (labelled) groups. Real data can then be modified by taking steps in latent space.

Application: Disentanglement

Aims for coordinate directions to correspond to real-world properties, often by regularization \[\begin{align*} L = - ELBO(\theta,\phi) + \lambda_1\mathbb{E}_{P(\mathbf{x})}[r_1[q(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x},\theta)]] + \lambda_2 r_2\left[\frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=1}^N q(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x}_i,\theta)\right] \end{align*}\]

\(\beta\)-VAE

\[\begin{align*} ELBO(\theta,\phi) \approxeq \log P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z}^*,\phi) - \beta D_{KL}(q(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x},\theta)||P(\mathbf{z})) \end{align*}\]

With spherical prior \(P(\mathbf{z}\), \(\beta>1\) encourages disentanglement by encouraging less correlated posterior.

Diffusion Models

Prince (2023, chap. 18)

Diffusion Models

  • Goal is to learn decoder that is inverse of a predetermined encoder.
  • Loss function encourages each network to invert the corresponding encoder step.
  • Can be viewed as (special case of) VAEs with hierarchical priors.

Encoder (Forward/Diffusion Process)

Markov chain \[\begin{align*} q(\mathbf{z}_1|\mathbf{x}) &= \mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{z}_1} \left( \sqrt{1-\beta_1} \mathbf{x}, \beta_1 \mathbf{I}\right) \\ q(\mathbf{z}_t|\mathbf{z}_{t-1}) &= \mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{z}_t} \left( \sqrt{1-\beta_t} \mathbf{z}_{t-1}, \beta_t \mathbf{I} \right) \qquad \forall t \in 2,\dots, T \end{align*}\] where \(\beta_t\) determine the noise schedule.

Diffusion Kernel \(q(\mathbf{z}_t|\mathbf{x})\)

\[\begin{align*} q(\mathbf{z}_t|\mathbf{x}) = \mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{z}_t} \left( \sqrt{\alpha_t} \mathbf{x}, (1-\alpha_t) \mathbf{I} \right) \end{align*}\] where \(\alpha_t = \prod_{s=1}^t (1-\beta_s)\).

Marginal Distributions \(q(\mathbf{z}_t)\)

\[\begin{align*} q(\mathbf{z}_t) = \int q(\mathbf{z}_t|\mathbf{x}) P(\mathbf{x}) d\mathbf{x} \end{align*}\] where \(P(\mathbf{x})\) not explicitly known.

Conditional Distribution \(q(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{z}_t)\)

\[\begin{align*} q(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{z}_t) = \frac{q(\mathbf{z}_t|\mathbf{z}_{t-1})q(\mathbf{z}_{t-1})}{q(\mathbf{z}_t)} \end{align*}\] is intractable because of the unknown marginals, but often well-approximated by normal distribution.

Cond. Diffusion Distribution \(q(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{z}_t,\mathbf{x})\)

Conditional on \(\mathbf{x}\) we do have closed form \[\begin{align*} q(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{z}_t,\mathbf{x}) = \mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{z}_{t-1}} \left( \frac{1-\alpha_{t-1}}{1-\alpha_t}\sqrt{1-\beta_t}\mathbf{z}_t+\frac{\sqrt{\alpha_{t-1}}\beta_t}{1-\alpha_t}\mathbf{x}, \frac{\beta_t(1-\alpha_{t-1})}{1-\alpha_t} \mathbf{I} \right) \end{align*}\] which will be used to train decoder.

Decoder (Reverse Process)

Given diffusion process, learn reverse process (approximating \(q(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{z}_t)\) with normal distributions) \[\begin{align*} P(\mathbf{z}_T) &= \mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{z}_T}(0,\mathbf{I}) \\ P(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{z}_t,\phi_t) &= \mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{z}_{t-1}} \left( \mathbf{f}_t[\mathbf{z}_t,\mathbf{\phi}_t], \sigma_t^2 \mathbf{I} \right) \\ P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z}_1,\phi_1) &= \mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{x}} \left( \mathbf{f}_1[\mathbf{z}_1,\mathbf{\phi}_1], \sigma_1^2 \mathbf{I} \right) \end{align*}\] Can generate from \(P(\mathbf{x})\) using ancestral sampling.

Deriving ELBO & Diffusion Loss

\[\begin{align*} \hat{\phi}_{1,\dots,T} &= \arg\max_{\phi_{1,\dots,T}} \sum_{i=1}^I \log P(\mathbf{x}_i|\phi_{1,\dots,T}) \\ &= \arg\max_{\phi_{1,\dots,T}} \sum_{i=1}^I \log \left( \int P(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T}|\phi_{1,\dots,T}) d\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T} \right) \end{align*}\] The marginalization renders max-likelihood intractable, so we derive ELBO where encoder distribution \(q(\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T}|\mathbf{x})\) is now predetermined (no \(\theta\)). Decoder chooses \(\phi\) to simultaneously tighten bound (so static encoder approximates \(P(\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T}|\mathbf{x},\phi_{1,\dots,T})\)) and optimize with respect to it.

\[\begin{align*} & \log(P(\mathbf{x}|\phi_{1,\dots,T})) \\ &= \log\left(\int q(\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T}|\mathbf{x}) \frac{P(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T}|\phi_{1,\dots,T})}{q(\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T}|\mathbf{x})} d\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T} \right) \\ &\geq \int q(\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T}|\mathbf{x}) \log\left(\frac{P(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T}|\phi_{1,\dots,T})}{q(\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T}|\mathbf{x})}\right) d\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T} =: ELBO(\phi_{1,\dots,T})\\ &= \int q(\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T}|\mathbf{x}) \left[\log\left(\frac{P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z}_1,\phi_1)}{q(\mathbf{z}_1|\mathbf{x})}\right) + \log\left(\frac{\prod_{t=2}^T P(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{z}_t,\phi_t)}{\prod_{t=2}^T \frac{q(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{z}_{t},\mathbf{x})q(\mathbf{z}_t|\mathbf{x})}{q(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{x})}}\right) + \log\left(P(\mathbf{z}_T)\right) \right] d\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T} \\ &= \int q(\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T}|\mathbf{x}) \left[\log\left(P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z}_1,\phi_1)\right) + \sum_{t=2}^T \log\left(\frac{ P(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{z}_t,\phi_t)}{q(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{z}_{t},\mathbf{x})}\right) + \underset{\approxeq 0}{\underbrace{\log\left(\frac{P(\mathbf{z}_T)}{q(\mathbf{z}_T|\mathbf{x})} \right) }}\right] d\mathbf{z}_{1,\dots,T} \\ &\approxeq \mathbb{E}_{q(\mathbf{z}_1|\mathbf{x})}[\log(P(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z}_1,\phi_1))] - \sum_{t=2}^T \mathbb{E}_{q(\mathbf{z}_t|\mathbf{x})}[D_{KL}(q(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{z}_t,\mathbf{x})||P(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{z}_t,\phi_t))] \end{align*}\] which results in minimization of the diffusion loss function (again using MC) \[\begin{align*} \min_{\phi_{1,\dots,T}} \sum_{i=1}^I \left[-\log\left(\mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{x}_i}(\mathbf{f}_1[\mathbf{z}_{i1},\phi_1], \sigma_1^2 \mathbf{I})\right) + \sum_{t=2}^T \frac{1}{2\sigma_t^2} \left|\left| \frac{1-\alpha_{t-1}}{1-\alpha_t}\sqrt{1-\beta_t}\mathbf{z}_{it} + \frac{\sqrt{\alpha_{t-1}}\beta_t}{1-\alpha_t}\mathbf{x}_i - \mathbf{f}_t[\mathbf{z}_{it},\phi_t] \right|\right|^2 \right] \end{align*}\] where \(\mathbf{z}_{it}\) is the latent var. correspond. to datapoint \(\mathbf{x}_i\) at diffusion step \(t\).

Reparametrized Loss Function

Since \(\mathbf{z}_t = \sqrt{\alpha_t}\mathbf{x} + \sqrt{1-\alpha_t}\mathbf{\varepsilon}_t\) we use model \(\hat{\varepsilon} = \mathbf{g}_t[\mathbf{z}_t,\phi_t]\) predicting noise (instead of predicting \(\hat{\mathbf{z}}_{t-1} = \mathbf{f}_t[\mathbf{z}_t, \phi_t]\)) and minimize \[\begin{align*} \min_{\phi_{1,\dots,T}} \sum_{i=1}^I \sum_{t=1}^T \left|\left| \mathbf{g}_t[\underset{\mathbf{z}_{it}}{\underbrace{ \sqrt{\alpha_t}\mathbf{x}_i + \sqrt{1-\alpha_t}\mathbf{\varepsilon}_{it}}},\phi_t] - \mathbf{\varepsilon}_{it} \right|\right|^2 \end{align*}\]
